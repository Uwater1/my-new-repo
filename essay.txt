### **The Concept of Divergence**

Divergence occurs when the price of an asset and a technical indicator (typically an oscillator) move in opposite directions.

* 
**Momentum Focus:** Using George Laneâ€™s Stochastic Oscillator, the strategy tracks price momentum, which typically changes direction before the price itself.


* **Bullish (Buy Side) Divergence:**
* 
**Stage 1:** Price makes a lower low while the stochastic is under 20. Then, the stochastic bounces back above 20.


* 
**Stage 2:** Price makes a new lower low (or double bottom), but the stochastic holds above 20 and turns up, creating a higher low.




* **Bearish (Sell Side) Divergence:**
* 
**Stage 1:** Price makes a higher high while the stochastic is over 80. Then, the stochastic pulls back below 80.


* 
**Stage 2:** Price makes a new higher high (or double top), but the stochastic holds under 80 and turns down, creating a lower high.





### **Technical Setup and HPS Methodology**

The "High Probability Setup" (HPS) Methodology relies on a combination of indicators to identify "HPS Zones":

* 
**Moving Averages:** 20, 50, and 200 Exponential Moving Averages (EMA).


* 
**Stochastic Settings:** Fast Stochastics (14,3 and 40,4) and Full Stochastic (60,10,10).


* 
**Chart Patterns:** Wedges, channels, 1-2-3 patterns, flags, and pennants.


* 
**Reversal Candlesticks:** Hammers, Piercing Patterns, Engulfing Patterns, Shooting Stars, and Tweezers.


* 
**Trade Confirmation:** Ideally, divergence signals should line up with channel lines (trend lines) and be capped with reversal candles.
